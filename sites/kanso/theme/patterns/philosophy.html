— 003 / Philosophy · 哲学

Seven principles
we return to.

簡素

Kanso — Simplicity

Eliminate what does not serve. Every element earns its presence in the room; clarity is sophistication.

自然

Shizen — Naturalness

Materials should be honest. Wood feels like wood; stone carries weight. We never disguise a material as something it is not.

間

Ma — Negative Space

The space between objects matters as much as the objects. We design the pauses that give a room its rhythm.

寂

Sabi — Patina of Time

We honor grain, oxidation, and the marks of making. Time improves what we build; nothing emerges fully resolved.

渋い

Shibui — Quiet Beauty

We prefer the work that reveals itself on the second look. Nothing demands attention on arrival.

幽玄

Yūgen — Suggestive Depth

A doorway half-lit, a view half-framed. What is withheld invites the mind inward.

禅

Zen — Inhabited Stillness

The room holds you. The body settles before the mind does.
