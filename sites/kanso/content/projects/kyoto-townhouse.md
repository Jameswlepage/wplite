A traditional Kyoto machiya — long, narrow, with a central courtyard — had stood mostly untouched for over a century. Our clients wanted to live in it without erasing it.

## Brief

Retain the timber structure and tile roof. Introduce modern services where they can remain invisible. Add a second bathroom. Fix the thermal envelope without changing how the house looks from the street.

## Approach

We rebuilt the floor substrate to accommodate underfloor heating and acoustic insulation while re-using every salvageable original plank. Services route through a new service spine hidden behind an existing earthen wall. A tea-ceremony room was restored to its original proportions using materials sourced from the same Kyoto craftsmen whose ancestors may have built it.

## Result

From the street, nothing has changed. Inside, the house is now warm in winter, cool in summer, quiet when the city is not. Our clients report sleeping through traffic noise for the first time in twenty years of city living.
