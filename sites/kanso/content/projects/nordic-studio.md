A twelve-person design agency needed a space that supported both solitary concentration and spontaneous collaboration — without the acoustic chaos of open-plan or the isolation of private offices.

## Brief

The team was losing productive hours to context-switching. Their previous office had no acoustic separation, harsh lighting, and nowhere to retreat for focused work. They wanted warmth, not corporate sterility.

## Approach

We designed three acoustic zones connected by a central timber spine — a long bench-and-shelf element that acts as wayfinding, storage, and informal meeting point. Individual focus rooms are lined in felt and white oak, with controlled natural light. The communal studio uses exposed concrete, large worktables, and pendant lighting that can be dimmed by zone. A tea kitchen anchors the transition between focus and collaboration spaces.

## Result

The agency reports a measurable increase in deep-work hours and a decrease in meeting frequency. Team members self-regulate between focus and communal zones without management overhead. The space has become a recruiting advantage.
