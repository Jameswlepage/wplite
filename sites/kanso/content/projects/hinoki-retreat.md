A private client building a bath house in the Hakone mountains needed materials that could withstand humidity and temperature extremes while aging beautifully over decades.

## Brief

The architect specified hinoki cypress for the soaking tub and wall panels, volcanic stone for the floor, and handmade ceramic tile for the changing area. Each material needed to be sourced from specific regions and certified for sustainable harvest.

## Approach

We worked with a third-generation hinoki mill in Kiso Valley to select old-growth timber with tight grain and high oil content — essential for water resistance. The volcanic stone was sourced from a quarry near Mount Aso and hand-split to preserve natural surface texture. Ceramic tiles were commissioned from a Shigaraki potter who fires in a traditional anagama kiln, producing subtle color variation across each piece.

## Result

Every material arrived on site with provenance documentation and care instructions. The bath house has been in use for six months with no warping, staining, or degradation. The hinoki has developed a warm golden patina that deepens with each season.
