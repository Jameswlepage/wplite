A ceramicist with a growing international following needed a Tokyo base — a working studio on one floor and a public-facing gallery on another, with a careful acoustic and visual separation between them.

## Brief

The studio floor should read as a workshop: durable, cleanable, tolerant of clay dust and water. The gallery floor should read as a gallery: white-walled, evenly lit, acoustically quiet. Both needed to feel like one building.

## Approach

A continuous travertine floor threads through the entrance, stair, and gallery landings — the spatial constant. The studio floor uses polished concrete, sealed but not glossy, with a sloped drain hidden under a low timber bench. The gallery uses a polished plaster wall system we developed with a local plasterer over six prototypes. A central staircase is the only shared vertical element, lit from a skylight above.

## Result

The ceramicist's work now lives and is shown in a space calibrated for both — and collectors report the space itself has become part of the work.
