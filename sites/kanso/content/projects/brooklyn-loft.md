A converted industrial space with excellent bones and a terrible floor plan — the clients wanted a single generous family home, organized around the rituals of cooking and gathering.

## Brief

Keep the exposed timber beams and brick. Build a kitchen that can actually host a dinner for twelve. Create a quiet zone for two kids to do homework without retreating to their rooms. Avoid anything that reads as "industrial chic."

## Approach

The kitchen became the organizing room — a single long stone-topped island flanked by a walk-in pantry on one side and a plaster-hearth banquette on the other. Children's study zones are inset into the window bays. All new partitions are full-height but demountable, so the plan can evolve as the kids grow up.

## Result

The space now feels neither industrial nor precious — it feels lived in. The family reports hosting Sunday dinners for twelve or more every other week.
