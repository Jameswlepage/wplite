— On keeping this journal

Colophon

Field Notes is a travelogue of small observations from specific places. Each entry begins as a handwritten note in a Field Notes №48 memo book, usually finished on the ground; it is transcribed later with weather data and coordinates.

The site is built on wplite — a flat-schema, AI-agreeable modern tier that compiles to a real WordPress plugin and block theme. Notes live as markdown files in the repository and round-trip through the database.

Type

Display: Caveat, a contemporary casual script by Impallari Type. Body: EB Garamond, a digital revival of Claude Garamont's 16th-century roman. Mono: JetBrains Mono, for coordinates, dates, and marginalia.

Colors

The palette is drawn from real journal materials — manila kraft, ruled cream paper, iron-gall ink, sumi black, and the rust of old stamps.
