Five centuries of an industry hanging on frames older than most countries I've visited.

## Conditions

Late winter. The wind off the Vestfjorden is specifically arranged to do this job: cold enough the fish doesn't rot, dry enough the fish doesn't spoil, steady enough the fish cures evenly. No one designed it. It just happens to be the right weather.

## Observation

I had expected the racks to smell. They don't — not at ten meters, not at two. The wind strips odor molecules faster than they can be released. Stand downwind and you catch a thin salt ghost of it and that's all.

The fish hang head-to-tail in pairs, tied with sisal. When one of them turns in a gust it makes a hollow wooden knock. Thousands of them. The sound was what I came home with.

## Note to Self

Record it next time. A thousand cod in the wind is the closest thing I've heard to an instrument.
