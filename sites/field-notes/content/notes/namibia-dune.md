The ridge is a meter wide at the top and you walk it like a tightrope made of iron-rich sand.

## Conditions

Left camp at 4:40. Half-moon. The east face begins to turn orange about thirty minutes before the sun crests the horizon. For a window of maybe six minutes, the dune is lit entirely by scattered blue sky and reflected pre-dawn — the colors you get in no other landscape.

## Observation

The sand is 5 million years old and came from the Orange River. It is redder the older it is, because iron oxidizes slowly in the dry air. The youngest sand in the Namib is almost beige. Dune 45 is somewhere in the middle, and in that window of light you can see the grading — the west side bluer, the east side already firing.

As the sun hit the ridge the sand I was standing on shifted a centimeter under its own weight and made a low note. A musician with a good ear could have named it.

## Note to Self

Bring a recorder. Also: slower. The whole point is slower.
