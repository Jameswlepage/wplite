I have been under dark sky before. This is not that.

## Conditions

New moon. No clouds. No nearby town. The host killed the porch light at 9:30 and said: "Let your eyes forget."

## Observation

Twenty minutes in, the Milky Way stopped being a band and became a structure. I could see the dark lanes — dust, not absence. The Magellanic Clouds were obvious enough that a child would point at them. Satellites crossed constantly, too many to count. Every thirty seconds, something fell.

Altitude and dryness explain the optics. The thing they don't explain is what it does to you. I sat in a camp chair and my pulse slowed. Nothing moved on the ground.

## Note to Self

Next time: no camera for the first hour. Just look.
