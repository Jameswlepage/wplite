The rules here are strict: you write a sutra before you're allowed in. The garden isn't a spectacle, it's a discipline.

## Conditions

It rained for twenty minutes as I was copying characters. By the time I finished the garden was steaming.

## Observation

Moss colors are not moss colors. They are the colors of moss plus water plus the angle of light through canopy. When the humidity is high and the sun comes back out, the garden looks lit from inside — tiny leaves pumping water, each one its own small lens. The golden pond darkened against the brightened moss and the contrast was sharper than any photograph will admit.

The priest who let me in afterwards said: "Come when it rains. Don't come when it doesn't."

## Note to Self

This is the clearest instance I have of a place being fundamentally a weather system, not a location.
