Arrived before sunrise. The lighthouse was gone inside a cloud that was still climbing.

## Conditions

The road up was clear. The tops of the hills were clear. Only a strip — maybe 80 meters thick — held fog, and the strip was moving up the slope instead of in from the sea.

## Observation

This is advection fog reshaped by terrain: sea-cooled air, denser than the warm air above, finding the lowest line it can follow. The coast here pinches the wind and the hill serves as a ramp. The fog behaves like a slow liquid — it pools in swales and stretches thin across ridges before pouring over.

At 7:12 a wren called inside the fog. At 7:14 the fog was past the ridge and the wren was silent.

## Note to Self

Bring the 85mm next time, not the 35. The interesting picture was the line where fog ended, not the fog itself.
